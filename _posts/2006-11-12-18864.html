I do not live in LA, but I have a friend who does and who homeschools there.
She has always told me it is absolutely wonderful after homeschooling in NY
for two years. She sends a letter to the Department of Education every fall
and that is it. No other reporting, no record keeping, no testing, no
interference. She regularly taunts me about this, as I'm still in NY, home
of heavy regulation (although we work around it just fine).

There is a LA unschooling yahoo group -
http://groups.yahoo.com/group/LAUnschoolers/. I'm sure you could get a
first hand answer there.

Suffering from state envy,

Alyssa,

Louisiana has 2 options - homestudy and private school.

Homestudy is using curriculum at home and private school is
establishing your own 'school'. I personally do the private school
option. It requires an annual letter to the state, but nothing else.
And you don't have to 'register' till age 7.

Here is a good site:
http://www.la-home-education.com/la/intent.html

Feel free to write off list if you want to discuss.
