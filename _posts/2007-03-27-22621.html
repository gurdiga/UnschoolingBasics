She's right - it's about unschooling and how to move in the direction of
unschooling and respectful parenting, REGARDLESS of what an individual's
belief system is or is not. I *am* a Christian and don't see a problem
combining unschooling and my belief in Jesus. My responses to situations
posed are rooted in my belief system, as is everything I do every day
(or at least I try to). There's nothing yet that I've found that creates
a dichotomy between belief in Jesus and unschooling. There are groups
*specific to* Christian unschoolers - that's not this one. This is
unschooling Basics - belief system is irrelevant to someone who is
trying to get a handle on what unschooling is and how it works and what
it looks like day to day. My house will look different than Ren's
because we're different families with different belief systems BUT the
basic principles are the same - love, respect, trust, freedom (and
aren't they the bottom line principles of Christianity anyhow?)

Deb
