School collectivists may look back on Friday, January
13, 2006 as "Black Friday."

20/20 is planning to run a one-hour special featuring
John Stossel. He interviews government officials, union
leaders, parents and students and makes suggestions
about how the system can be improved (w/o, I fear,
even honorable mention for the only real solution,
ending government involvement in education attendance,
content, financing, and regulation).

"Stupid In America: How We Are Cheating Our Kids" with
John Stossel airs Jan. 13 (10 to 11 p.m. ET) on ABC.
See below how to order a copy or transcript.

In the words of E Ray Moore, good friend and president
of Exodus Mandate:

"Now Stossel and 20/20 are set to puncture the last
myth of complacent suburban parents who delude themselves
by thinking that, whatever problems there may be elsewhere,
their schools are "different." As Stossel and 20/20
point out, not only do our 12th graders do worse in
math and science, for example, than students in almost
all other industrialized countries, the longer our
students are in public schools, the worse they do in
relation to their international peers."

While I hate to quibble with my friend Rev. Ray, I
suspect it's NOT the last myth. The "public school"
establishment is certainly adept at making up new myths
when the public sniffs the rat in the current myth
du jour.
