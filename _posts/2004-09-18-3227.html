The Center for Unschooling Information, as its very first project, has
opened new message boards at: <_http://www.unschooling.info/forum>_
(http://www.unschooling.info/forum>)

We are excited to announce the creation of these new boards, which are
devoted to discussion of anything and everything related to unschooling.

Discussions will include such topics as: Getting Started Unschooling,
Deschooling, Supporting Kids' Endeavors, Finding Support, and much, much more.
These boards are easy to search and are conveniently organized to allow on-topic,
in-depth discussion.

You may be wary about utilizing message boards. But, these boards are loaded
with features that make navigation a breeze. For instance, you can sign up
to get new messages sent to your e-mail address. You can edit or delete your
own posts. You can add a hyperlink to your posts, track all activity since
your last visit, and search posts for specific topics. There is even a FAQ to
help you learn about all the forum features and how to use them:
<_http://www.unschooling.info/forum/faq.asp#format>_
(http://www.unschooling.info/forum/faq.asp#format>)

Please join this new online community at:
<_http://www.unschooling.info/forum>_ (http://www.unschooling.info/forum>)

The Center for Unschooling Information
<_www.unschooling.info>_ (http://www.unschooling.info>/)

I think that HEM isn't really promoting unschooling anymore - that they
don't want unschooling to be considered something different than other
homeschooling. I think there are enough of us radicals that aren't in that same boat.
Unschooling IS different and sometimes even so FAR from school-at-home that
we're not even in the same swimming hole.

That the boards were down for so long was disturbing to me----not that they
may have been working feverishly to get them back up---who knows? But I'm
glad that there's a new place for folks to go that is owned and run by people
