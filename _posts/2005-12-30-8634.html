Perhaps there needs to be a "carnival of radical unschooling." I
visited that carnival a few days ago, and some of the links didn't
strike me as especially good examples of unschooling--like the woman
who called in an "unschooling day" when her carefully structured
plans for her kids' education fell apart for some reason. Or this,
from the "explaining what we do all day" post:

"The day after Halloween is the day the children get all the candy
out of their bags they want, all day, Milky Way after Skittles, after
KitKat, after Reece's after Pixie Sticks, for tomorrow, what they did
not gorge on today, goes away. Helps me not have to regulate it from
now til Easter."

Su
