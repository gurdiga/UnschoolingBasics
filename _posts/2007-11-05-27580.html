Try Maple Story http://maplestory.nexon.net
<http://maplestory.nexon.net> . They might also want to visit Imbee at
http://imbee.com <http://imbee.com> . Imbee is a social networking site
for the tween crowd, sort of the next step after Club Penguin.

I recommend that you visit Gamesparentsteachers.com at
http://www.gamesparentsteachers.com
<http://www.gamesparentsteachers.com> to find tools aimed at
encouraging a conversation about games between kids and parents. This
is one of Marc Prensky's sites. He is the author of "Don't Bother Me
Mom - I'm Learning!" which argues that electronic game playing is
beneficial to "digital native" kids.

I've also been posting a series of articles on Wired Learning on my
Living By Learning blog site at
http://www.onlivingbylearning.blogspot.com
<http://www.onlivingbylearning.blogspot.com> . The latest article is
titled Explore The Wired World With Your Kids
<http://onlivingbylearning.blogspot.com/2007/11/explorethe-wired-world-w\
ith-your-kids.html> , and it has a list of suggestions for surfing the
internet.

Bon voyage on the digital highway, Sandra
