I keep reading accounts of people who have families don't support the
decision to unschool (or even homeschool), and I think those families need
desperately to get themselves to at least one (it will probably be more -- they're
addictive!) Unschooling conference. And I'm not just saying that because I am
coordinating one.

There are a lot of reasons. You'll develop a tribe that will keep you sane
when you're getting negative messages from your family. If you go through a
hard time, you'll know people who can support you. You'll meet older kids that
will show you that you are, indeed, making a responsible choice for your
children's learning life. You'll develop (and your kids will) a confidence in
talking about unschooling that may just win your family over.

Check out: _Live and Learn Conference_ (http://liveandlearnconference.org/)
in New Mexico this year
_Toronto Unschooling Conference Home_
(http://www.livingjoyfully.ca/conference/index.htm) in Toronto
Northeast Unschooling Conference _index.html_
(http://northeastunschoolingconference.com/) in Massachusetts

Kathryn

Come to the Northeast Unschooling Conference, Memorial Day Weekend, May
26-28, 2006 in Peabody, Massachusetts! www.NortheastUnschoolingConference.com

I second that. Also, you can feed them (family and friends) info in small
bites. My parents didn't understand attachment parenting. I would just say
well I'm giving her roots. They were afraid I was isolating her by always
having her with me. I assured them I would give her wings and thow her out
of the nest.

My parents came to visit when Lyndsey was 3 1/2. As they were leaving my mom
said I was doing a great job. My dad and I both dropped our jaws. Sometimes
it just takes time to see what you believe.
