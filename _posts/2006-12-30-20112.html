No, No, NO!
Md law does not require ANY of the above. Please head to NHEN.org and read up on the laws.
A portfolio of samples of work is all that is needed. No attendance, no teaching supply list, you don't have to show "dated work". You do not have to show progress. You must show that you provide "a regular and thourough method of instruction".
Please know your state laws and don't give them anything more than that.
Best of all, Md. allows for an umbrella school option in which you avoid the county school district completely.
