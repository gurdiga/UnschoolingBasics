Hi There,

I just wanted to thank those of you who replied to my recent post about my
concerns and anxieties about unschooling.... they have made a real
difference. I can feel a subtle yet important shift in where I am at with
it in my head.... like I can feel a shift in the weight of responsibility
from me to my son for his education.

I think I have started noticing a little more about where he is at... ie: a
friends schooled daughter stayed the night recently and Daniel helped her
with her maths homework. He is 11, she is 14.

I want to have some more conversations with him about his being responsible
for his education, and me being an enthusiastic supporter of whatever he is
into. I was wondering if there are any articles that I could point him to
that maybe have been written by unschooled teens to explain the unschooling
philosophy to him so that he can get a better fix on what I am trying to do
with him. I think I need to "come out of the closet" with him! It into
that I have been hiding there, but I guess because I have not been clear in
myself what I want to do here, I havn;t had that conversation with him
either. All I know is that everytime I read any unschooling stuff I just
get a big YES inside, and that's because it feels so natural.

Thanks to Pam (I think?) for those book recommendations. I have ordered "The
Book of Learning and Forgetting", and have dug up my Mary Griffin book, and
my Grace Lewellyn books too. I so wish I could get even ONE copy of
Growing without Schooling. Anyone have a copy they might send me just so I
could have a look?? I would send it back!

Warm thoughts
Hilary
