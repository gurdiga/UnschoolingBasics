"What's the difference between Rethinking
Education conference and Live and Learn conference?
They both seem to be aimed at unschoolers, but I
haven't heard RE being talked about much here or on
UD."

While each conference has it's own flavor, there are also some very
big differences between these, from what I've heard. Having only been
an L&L attendee, I can't give you first hand on RE.

I know the lady that runs it is a total unschooler (there was a great
article about her grown son recently) but I think they allow for a bit
more "stretch" in the topics presented. Some of the talks lean more
towards relaxed/eclectic I believe.
Also, L&L is geared to be very family inclusive. The funshops have all
ages attending and is very kid friendly. It gets a little hectic
sometimes too...very bubbly and loud and overwhelming at times, but
the kids are so involved in everything. I love it.

L&L is the only conference I know of, where ALL of the talks are about
unschooling and there is huge support for radical unschooling. It's
amazing to be surrounded by hundreds of people all treating kids
respectfully and as equals. My ATC funshop had ALL ages and the adults
were completely supportive of every single persons work. I've never
been in a community that supports kids with so much respect before.
It's pretty awesome. I'm biased of course.:)

Ren
