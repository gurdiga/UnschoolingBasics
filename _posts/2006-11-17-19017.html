Maggie, if your son is basically happy then you ARE doing right by
him - really!

> I'm happy and realize I'm lucky my son is basically happy and
seems
> well adjusted, I can't take the credit for it and hope that I'm
doing
> right by him.

If you are unschooling and parenting gently, then you probably won't
have many people that share your views, whether or not your child
has Asperger's. Our lifestyle is definitely not mainstream!

> Really just wanted a different take on things, wondered how others
> unschooled their children with asperger's. DH and I are feeling a
bit
> alone, our thoughts on things seem so different then the ones
around
> us. The unschooling life fits us so well, but we really don't
have
> people around that share our views.

I understand that in the world of special needs, it is even more
emphasized that certain children need specific help from experts,
etc. Schools get a lot of money for these special classes! They
want you to think that you can't do it yourself. But you don't have
to buy into that.

There are certainly unschoolers with special needs who do use
classes, therapy, and/or medication. It all depends on the child
and the need or problem. But if your son is happy, I totally
wouldn't worry about it. If there is ever a time when he isn't
happy anymore or is having a difficult time with some aspect of his
life, then I'm sure you will do what you can to help him get what he
needs.

-Christy M.
