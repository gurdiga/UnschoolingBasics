Well, I don't know....I thought it would be more
unschooly, but in reality there were just two
"unschooling" workshops, actually one and a Q&A. The
first workshop had only about 15 people in it, and it
was by Victoria Moran, who, although very nice,
thought that the TV was a demon box (I can never trust
a person who says that to *actually* be
unschooling--maybe it's just me ;). The Q&A was full
of people who "well, of course we can't trust our 13
year old home alone while we go to town....just think
what she might get into!" and "we have a TV, for after
the kids go to bed at night..." There were some
"real" unschoolers there, too, but it didn't feel like
they were as loudly voiced. As for the other
speakers? David Albert said things in his teen class
like "You should have some sort of ceremony so that
your kids understand they have more say in their
education now as they get older...." In one class the
speaker said she "got the kids up for school at
7"....Maybe I'm too overly sensitive (? been known to
happen!) but no, I would call this a secular
homeschooling convention with a few unschoolers thrown
in for good measure....But maybe others saw it
differently? Kelly, I think you don't have to worry
about any competition just yet ;) Until Deb and I get
the Montana Unschoolers conference off the ground :D
