It depends on what your state requirements are. Some states you simply
notify the school district of your intent to homeschool and you never need
do anything ever again. While other states have more stringent
requirements. It is important to know what your state requirements are. I
have found it very interesting that when some parents have notified their
districts of intent to homeschool the local district will make homeschooling
sound like it is difficult with tons of hoops to jump through and lots of
requirements. Yet, none of these hoops are required by the state. It's a
"scare tactic" to make parents think it would be just easier to keep their
child in the school system. Or they are made up by a school district
employee who doesn't know the state laws.

If you are living in a state that has lots of requirements, the best thing
to do is to look for an unschooling group in your state. I bet most of the
states have a "Your state Unschooling" yahoogroup. These families are
masters at how to word unschooling lifestyles into schoolish looking
jargon. :)
