Here, let me expound.

My daughter has dark auburn hair so we had to bleach it.
After bleaching it, I used a paint brush and painted the hair
henna on making sure I got it all.(pre-mixed from Henna king)
My daughter has extremely thick long hair so it took like six tubes.

When the roots would grow in, I'd bleach just the new hair growth
then henna'd them. The Henna intensifies with the more you put on
and it lasts for months.

First she went neon pink. It was so pink that a wild macaw spotted
her and came to visit. Her hair was so brillant that she could be spotted
over three quarters of a mile away. No joke. Actual measured distance.
http://s185.photobucket.com/albums/x79/geppetto24/Henna%20works/?action=view¤t=CIMG0008.jpg

Later she wanted to add other colors so I blended it from pink to deep
purple to black on the under layers.
http://s185.photobucket.com/albums/x79/geppetto24/Henna%20works/?action=view¤t=CIMG0567.jpg

When she finally tired of the bright hair, we bleached it once again (the pink returned some
so she looked like Naruto, lol) Then we colored it as close to her natural color and dread locked it.

http://www.dreadheadhq.com/

It took me eight hours just to section it all before I started dreading. So be forwarned, it's a big
process but she looked cute and got lots of compliments from other dreadheads+. When she tired
of dreads, she cut them out and is now sporting an adorable short do, ready to start her professional
training as a Firefighter. (She can't have 'fun' hair while in the Academy.
http://s185.photobucket.com/albums/x79/geppetto24/?action=view¤t=Caylee.jpg

Vicki
