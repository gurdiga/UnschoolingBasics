2007 Highlights: Debbie Shapiro
Unschooling = Montessori!

DEBBIE SHAPIRO
is a parent, step-parent and educator who, thanks to the support of her husband Sig, has enjoyed spending over twenty seven years playing with children. Although Debbie is new to un-schooling, she is very excited about it and shares the underlying values and philosophy. Debbie has over 22 years of direct personal experience combating the drill and test mentality that plagues traditional education. Her strong conflictual feelings on testing compelled her to close her highly successful Montessori pre-school program. She is now a successful, and much happier, Montessori school ³drop-out.² She remains a passionate lover of all things Montessori: hands-on, self-directed and fun. Each of her sessions will incorporate the best of Montessori theory, practice and materials.
Debbie holds a BA in Human Development; Certification as a California Early Childhood Education Teacher and is a credentialed Montessori Pre-Primary Teacher. She runs her own consulting practice; Evergreen Education Services.
Debbie will host:

Knowledge is always the best tool for making good choices. You will learn how to assess where your child is in his/her development and how to support their unique approach to learning. This knowledge will help guide you in selecting a full range of creative, open-ended, self-directed activities. We¹ll talk about and experiment with many different kinds of activities which will compliment your child¹s learning styles, abilities and interests.

About Rethinking Education
Founded in 1994, Rethinking Education, with its international conference, is at the forefront of a rapidly growing trend in education.
RE is organized and produced by Barb Lundgren, an unschooling mom with 24 years of magical, glorious and utterly amazing unschool experience. What a great life it is to nurture and support not only your child's drive to be free, but your own as well!
At the heart of Rethinking Education is awareness that children are supremely capable of absorbing and using knowledge from our complex world. There is no need for arbitrary structure in education; the use of coercion, rewards or other behavior modification techniques as motivation are counterproductive. With freedom, respect and nurturing support, children have a powerful drive to self-direct their own learning; the result being children who direct their own education... indeed, their own futures.
Education viewed this way becomes a mutual endeavor, a life-long process of questioning and discovery ... a true exploration of self, society, our big, beautiful world.
Proponents are widespread and include John Taylor Gatto, Jan Fortune-Wood, John Holt, Bruno Bettleheim, Alice Miller, Ivan Illich, Susannah Sheffer, A.S. Neill, Daniel Greenberg, Jonathan Kozol, and many others.

MORE 2007 Highlights: Michael Mendizza
Welcome back Michael!

Play is the most serious undertaking of a childs life. It is the umbrella under which all learning takes place.
Joseph Chilton Pearce
MICHAEL MENDIZZA
is an author, educator, documentary filmmaker, and founder of Touch the Future, a nonprofit learning design center, and executive producer of the Nurturing Project. Michael¹s work centers on the state of optimum learning and performance, what Athletes call the Zone, researchers call Flow and children call Play. Both Nurturing and Touch the Future promote authentic free play as the optimum state to meet any challenge, at any age. Helping adults rediscover and embody this state while relating with children eliminates the majority of conflicts in a relationship, freeing energy and attention required for optimum learning and performance. Michael writes and speaks on varied topics including Media and the Brain, The Next Frontier in Education, Birth, Bonding & Brain Development, Transcendent Mentoring of This and Future Generations, Who Do We Think We Are (on the origins of self and its limitations), Science, Story, and Creativity, The Origins of Love and Violence and The
Intelligence of Play. He is the author of Magical Parent-Magical Child, a book he co-authored with Joseph Chilton Pearce. His websites are www.ttfuture.org, and www.nurturing.us
Michael will host the following sessions this year:
To Me Or Not To Me
Ever feel embarrassed? Why? The images we have about ourselves predetermine how we relate to the world and most importantly to our children. If the our self-image image is switched on, smart, stupid, fat, thin, boy, girl, macho, cute, sexy, our behavior is filtered through that image. Our behavior is limited and we infect our children with the same limitations. Collectively we call these limiting filters ME. For most of us, for most of our lives we think that this collective image called is real and necessary. In optimum states, play, flow and what athletes call the zone, the limiting self-image is switched off, freeing the full spectrum of our vast potential to meet the world fully and completely. As parents and educators, To Me or Not to ME? That is indeed is the question if we want our children to actualize their full potential.
Anger, Conflict and General ARRGHs: a Dad's Discussion Group
Michael's research and life experience has convinced him that much, perhaps even the majority, of men's general grumbling, anger and dissatisfaction is due to identity conflicts between them and the women in their early lives, beginning very early with dear old-mom. Want to hear more? Share your own ideas, and explore resolutions.
