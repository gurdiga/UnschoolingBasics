Pamela Sorooshian

27 May 2008, at 12:59pm

Join HSC - (HomeSchool Association of California) - it is the most
unschooling-friendly of the state organizations.
<HSC.org>

Call or email the HSC county contact and ask about making local
connections.
<http://hsc.org/countycontacts.html#sa>

Join the HSC email list and post that you're looking for contacts and
activities.
To join, send a blank email to:
<ca-Homeschooling-HSC-subscribe@yahoogroups.com>

If you like to camp, be sure to go to the HSC campouts - there are
always a couple not too far from SLO every year - Refugio State Beach
and Big Sur, for example.

Homeschoolers of the Central Coast looks like it is a currently active
group and is relaxed and casual.
<http://groups.yahoo.com/group/Homeschoolers_of_the_Central_Coast/>

Welcome to California. SLO is a great place to live!


-Pam

holistic_living

28 May 2008, at 2:11pm

It got cold here last weekend but it is warming up. I think it may
still be too cold for slide rock but anywhere along the river will be
nice for hiking around. My son is also a gamer but loves to go to the
trout farm and fish. It can get pricey, you have to pay for the fish
you catch and can't catch and release, but its a blast for the kids
and exciting as you can see them. There is also Page Springs Hatchery
which is neat to visit. Take a drive up the canyon toward Flag and
you can stop along the river and hike down along it, that's always
nice. My homeschooling (mostly unschooling) group meets at Sunset
Parker on Thursdays starting at 11:00. Kids are ages 5 up to 13. Its
casual and fun. Turn east at the Walgreens on Sunset and go just a
little ways, you'll see the park on your right. Feel free to join us
and get some more ideas from others there.

Janice
