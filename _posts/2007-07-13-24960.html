I went to RE in 2004, and I've been to two L&L since then (St. Louis & Abq). RE wasn't really an unschooling conference. That year it was a 3-day affair (Fri-Sun) with Friday being almost completely presentations by curriculum reps. The vendor area was filled with curriculum dealers all weekend. There were a couple of non-curriculum tables, but not with the completely unschooling feel of L&L.

There was a box-town kinda play area for little kids and some other kid stuff, but since I'd gone alone -- the kids were with dh and my sister's family while I went conferencing -- I don't really know much about that part of it.

Overall, I like L&L much better.

Hi there,

I went to RE last year, and have never been to L&L (but it sounds very
interesting!). I was only really just getting into unschooling when we
went, AND since my kids were 3 & 4 at the time, I wasn't really looking at
curriculum. I second the statement that Barb Lundgren is a die-hard
unschooler (this year they will be showing a preview of the movie "Schooled"
- previews look good). I also remember feeling very uncomfortable at how
free the kids were allowed to be. I felt like I was free-falling sometimes
(those kids should be sitting down or just leave -- wait, wait, breathe,
it's OK.) Yes, there were "experts" and it definitely was a "conference" --
at least for me, as I went to as many meetings as I could. But for me,
those meetings did more for me for unschooling and RU (though I didn't know
it at the time) than most other things I've read since (haven't been able to
go anywhere else, sadly).

Last year I worked in the Kids' Village at RE. The area for them was huge,
I think it took up three of the hotel's conferene areas (movable dividers).
The workers there appeared to be much less dominating and directive than
other child care-givers I've used. The tone also appeared to be that you
could bring in your kid IF you wanted to. There were a number of children
in some of the meetings, but there were also a number of things for kids to
do (up through teens) IF they wanted to. I think there was a perpetual
group of teens just hanging out in one area in the atrium(?). The kids were
(to me at the time) essentially allowed to go where they wanted to and do
what they wanted to. but there was stuff scheduled for them if they were
interested.

I think the biggest relief to me was the large amount of DADS that
attended. I was really quite worried about attending this huge
estrogen-fest. We had a Dad's Meeting on Sat. night which was also really
helpful for me. I'm hoping there'll be even more dads there this year.

--Rob Saxon
DH to Seana for 10 years
"Daddy!" to Genevieve (5.5) and Elissa (4+)

--Rob
