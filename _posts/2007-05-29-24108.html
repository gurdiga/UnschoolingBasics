Hi folks!
I am in the process of beginning to plan for one, and possibly two,
unschooling conferences for the next year.

First, I am planning a return of the Northeast Unschooling Conference. I
believe this would be around Memorial Day like last time, and would be a
conference in the Live and Learn model.

I want to get input into another possibility, however. We have just had a
really cool thing open near us. As part of a hotel, they have opened an indoor
water park. It sounds amazing. I was thinking it might make a great
mini-conference idea. We probably wouldn't have much in the way of speakers -- maybe
one or two, and maybe a few funshops for non-watery sorts.It would be
primarily a social conference, one or two nights. I was thinking it might be a good
winter unschooling getaway. I have not spoken to the place yet, so I have no
idea how much it would cost.

What do you all think?

Kathryn Baptista

Kathryn,

I love the idea of a gathering with funshops and a few
planned activities. Perhaps something like the small
discussion groups - I forget what they were called.
But no speakers.

When I came to the conference last year, I managed to
attend only one talk because my son was miserable and
wanted me to stay in the hotel room with him. He does
not like crowds. Anyhow, I still had a good time
connecting "after-hours" with folks. So for me the
conference ended up being a social event. And one that
I enjoyed very much.

Manisha
