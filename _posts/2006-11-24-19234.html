Danielle Lewandowski, CHt

Hi Dawn,

Actually, in fairness, no I didn't. I read the blog by the person who
was there during the taping. I know, I know, I should have waited to
draw my own conclusions first. Having said that, I do feel healthy
discussion is good and that there is something positive about the
discussion even being had. I think the part that bothered me the most
was about the audience filling. It makes it challenging to have a
balanced discussion when the moderators rig the balance of the audience.

I've also heard/read that based on the initial response to the
original format of the show, that the producers somehow toned it down
before it actually aired. I don't know that to be true or not, but
the original airing was to have been October 27th and it was delayed
for whatever reason.

Also, my opinion is based on my own thoughts of Dr Phil himself.
Having a background in psychology and being a hypnotherapist myself, I
relate to him in perhaps a different way. I see that he often puts his
guests in a very defensive position and that is not helpful to anyone
in finding resolution to an issue.

So having said that, I do see that some good will come out of his
program in that the discussion is at least being had and that it has
come to the attention of many that homeschooling is becoming more and
more prevalent. If I weren't already homeschooling, my question would
be WHY? Why are so many choosing to homeschool now? Could it be that
perhaps the system, such as it is now, no longer serves it's purpose
for the benefit and betterment of all? I wouldn't pit homeschool vs
public school, I'd find out why so many educated and rational people
are choosing to go this route.

Danielle L.
