Of course, the vast MAJORITY of people were unschooled until the turn
of the 20th century! So your mom's grandparents and all their friends
were more than likely unschooled. <g>

John Holt started promoting "no school" about 1975-76. Before that he
was a school reformer. So the *young* children of the parents he was
reaching "became adults" in the late 1990s. The second "wave" is just
reaching adulthood now.

My son is 20 and, although not fully on board with unschooling when we
started, is a HUGE advocate now. He knows he can do anything.
Plus---he's seen his schooled friends struggling so.

He'd be happy to answer any specific questions you may have:

Unschooling has been around *forever*, it's schooling that hasn't! There's actually much more evidence throughout history that supports unschooling! Take a look sometime at the list of people that have not attended school that have changed the world as we know it. It helped me to learn that school was set up for a much different reason than it's used today, and it hasn't been around all that long. Long enough to do lots of damage to lots of kids, but not long from a historical perspective anyway. I know that isn't your particular question, just my two cents :o).
