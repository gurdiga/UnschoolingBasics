We don't live there nor will we be there <g> , but...

If you're looking for things to do:

Tarpon Springs is fun. A little Greek city with great food. They
sponge dive there, and have a few snazzy shops among all the trashy
touristy shops.

Just west of there is Palmetto, FL. There's a guide dog school
(SouthEastern Guide Dogs). In the afternoons, you can make an
appointment to hug puppies that are waiting to be placed with their
puppy raising families who will love and train them until they're ready
for guide dog training. Our Ryan was born and trained there after we
raised him. He now is a full-fledged guide dog for his new partner,
Jaimie. YAY!

We also went to Bush Gardens. The boys and I had a blast! Ben saw
Cirque du Soliel in downtown Tampa. Tampa also has a cute walking
district with cute shops, art galleries, and restaurants.

Have fun!

<<Kelly wrote: Tarpon Springs is fun. A little Greek city with great food. They
sponge dive there, and have a few snazzy shops among all the trashy
touristy shops.>>

My bil has great shops in Tarpon Springs "Five Fish." There are two shops. One across from the shops and the other near the aquarium. This would fall in the category of "snazzy" stuff. Also - be sure to sample the great greek food in the neighborhood. Or the florida fruit wines (another shop of bil - near the aquarium.) There is a candle making shop near the aquarium that is also fun to visit.

If your looking for something fun to do - try letterboxing in St Petersburg. We found an old indian site that was nestled in a suburban neighborhood. Great short little adventure. Or one of my favorite places in St Petes is the St Petersburg Clay Company. http://www.stpeteclay.com/
There are also a few great needlepoint shops in St Petes. And the Dali Museum. Also the aquarium in Tampa is fun. One day is not enough time.

Have fun.
Lisa Heyman
