Last night I had a young lady come to the cafe who was dressed in lots
of black with chains, and spikey hair, stark make up, somewhat
gothy-metal with these really cool black and day-glo green tennis
shoes. She was wearing a t-shirt with the name of a band on it that
was ______ Butterfly. It was not Iron Butterfly (I would have
recognized that one) nor was it Butterfly Effect (ditto). We chatted
for a minute. I asked her about the band and she said those ill-fated
words, "You're too old to understand this kind of music." I SO wanted
to say, "Sweetie I was going to grunge and punk underground concerts
before your mama was out of diapers!" Instead I said, "Try me. What
kind of music do they play?" She hemmed and hawed and so I said, "Is
it interstitial?" (Total blank stare) "Punk?" "Not really"
"Grunge?" "No not that either." "Heavy metal?" "No" "Techno?
Acid? Head banging? Pop? Garage?" "No. No. No. No. No. It's hard
to define." "Oh, so interstitial." (another blank stare.) So I told
her that I would look them up and hear what they had.

Well, now I am home and in front of the computer and can't remember
the first part of the name. The logo was a butterfly with the name of
the band over the it and red "splattery blood/paint" across it. Does
this ring a bell with anyone? I figured this would be a good group to
ask as we have children that are all over the spectrum as far as music
tastes go.

Michelle - who refuses to adopt the label "old foagy" :-D
