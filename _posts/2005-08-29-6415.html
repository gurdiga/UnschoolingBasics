Haha! That's great! A bunch of our local unschooling groups have gotten a
sudden influx of Thomas Jefferson-pushing folks. It's weird and they seem
to be trying to recruit people at our park days. :o(

Anyway... I've not blogged about this yet, but my husband's father's wife
has invited us to Ireland. She asked about me, which was a first and we
were so excited about it that my daughter decided to learn Farsi. So, we've
been doing the Instant Immersion Farsi CD all day today (well, she outlasted
me by about 2 hours). Right now she's lying on the bed saying toilett e
khanumha kojast (where's the ladies' room?). :o)

The day before that, we slept in until 1:30, hung out and went to the park
at 5 to meet our unschooling buddies. Played until 10:15 until there were
no other people there, came home, ate a late dinner and watched TV Land
until 1:30. Then she read me a kid version of The Tempest. We finally
turned off the light at 2:30 and slept until 12:30 today. Well, she did. I
was up at 10.

We've been having a ton of fun with our new (to us) vintage blocks we got
from Ebay. We've been building tall structures and taking pictures of them.
We've also been playing a lot of Jenga, and we just start messing around
with a tangrams set.

Margaux (8) missed the release of Eldest, so that's on our "to do" list for
this weekend.

Our unschooling schedule is that we have no schedule at all. :o) And we
like it that way.

Nichole
