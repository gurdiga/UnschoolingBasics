even read to ds. I have taken to turning the tv on for her so he and I can
play a game or read.
Does anyone have any suggestions or ideas? The worry is large that poor ds is
not getting what he needs to enjoy himself right now.

Hi, Nancy,

Mine are not split as far apart as yours are (currently dd 5.7 and ds 3.4),
but we went through exactly the period you are describing and I went through
a big worry phase too around the 3 year mark with ds. We didn't have the
"going out" problem so much since my daughter prefers to be at home. But my
daughter started commenting on the fact that "You spend all your time
playing with Nicky!" She even asked me to limit the time I was playing with
him! What ended up helping was a couple of things. Firstly, we talked
about it quite a bit and I acknowledged that Nicky was going through a big
phase of wanting to play with mama all the time and this was a big switch
from when he wanted to just play with his big sister all the time. I told
her I could see how difficult this was for her and I encouraged her to tell
me at the point at which she wanted to have time with me (as opposed to
waiting for me to be "free" which is what she was doing endlessly). I also
started just randomly checking in with her throughout the day, asking "How
are you doing? Is there anything you want to do with mama?" If she said
"yes", I started asking my son to wait. I'd explain that Leenie needed me
to do such and such with her right now and suggest something he could do or
invite him to join us in some way. Sometimes he'd trot off and do something
else, sometimes he'd join us, sometimes he would just choose to nurse during
these periods and I'm glad he's still nursing since that helped him wait.
Wish I could offer you help on the going out part of it, but we have the
opposite situation here - 3 year old wants to go out more often and older
child is the confirmed introvert homebody.

Joan
