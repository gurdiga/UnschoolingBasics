We have taken eco-tours in Charleston a couple of times, and I
*highly* recommend that experience! The company we went through was
Barrier Island Eco-Tours. http://www.nature-tours.com/index.html They
took us to Capers Island, an undeveloped barrier island and nature
preserve, where they cooked out for us (yum!) and the kids could
wander around, and gave us a tour of the tidal creeks. They catch sea
life in a net, tell what they know about it, then release. (They have
a tank onboard) We did go with a group, I've never done it
individually. I *loved* the tour guides, they're all naturalists, and
they really made the trip fun. Worth *every* penny, then some.

We saw dolphins!
