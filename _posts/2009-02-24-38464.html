You'd need to know the *homeschooling* laws for where you live - they
vary by state (and in some places, by school district). Unschooling,
legally, is the same entity as homeschooling. So, if you need to file
paperwork to homeschool, file paperwork. Whatever is needed, no more.

Then take a LONG vacation. Seriously. Pretend summer vacation started
way early. What would you do? Go to the park? Visit a children's
museum? Snuggle up cozy with cocoa and a favorite movie? Do it. Don't
even THINK about school for at least as many months as your child has
been in school. Don't think about "subjects" like math or spelling.
Think back to when your child was 3 years old - you were living and
learning together hand over fist every day *without* subjects or
school. Live like that again.

--Deb

Adriana

There is a great group not far from you. We meet weekly in Long Beach
http://dragontree.vilter.us/ and if you are lucky Pam Sorooshian
http://www.sandradodd.com/pamsorooshian (a member of this list) will be
speaking. I haven't been in the last month but she was speaking weekly
prior to that. I've learned so much from being exposed to her talks that
I'm sure I haven't thanked her nearly enough. Thanks again Pam. The IRL
presentation of information and the sharing within the group at a park is
the perfect format for me to absorb information that I would have
ignored/missed in print. There are some other groups around but DT is the
"go to" group imho.

We are down at the southern tip of OC and find the 45m drive well worth the
effort.

Hope you find the irl support you are looking for. There are plenty of non
USing groups that are USing friendly that are around when you are ready.
Feel free to email me off list if you can't find them via yahoogroups or
google.

BTW I have a sister, now 21, who went to the Waldorf School of OC (I think
that's the name) through 4th grade. We considered Journey School, the
Waldorf inspired charter here in South County, when ds K aged. It was not a
good fit for him much to my mothers disapointment.
